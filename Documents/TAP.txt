IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
SOP: Module Structure & MDX Guidelines (The 6-Step Flow)
To maintain consistency across the platform, every module MDX file MUST strictly follow this exact 6-step architecture:
[Story] The Hook: Begin with a short, relatable narrative or scenario (1-2 paragraphs) explaining the core concept in plain language without technical jargon.
[Facts] The Grounding: Provide 3-4 bullet points of real-world data, financial loss statistics, or historical context to prove the threat is real.
[Interactive] The Engagement: Insert the interactive component (e.g., a simulated phishing email, a dark pattern UI toggle, or an OSINT tool embed).
[Unpack] The Analysis: Explain how the interactive example just worked. This section MUST include the Threat Taxonomy Block:
Vector: How is this attack delivered? (e.g., SMS, Social Media, Physical QR)
Motivation: What does the attacker want? (e.g., Financial, Data Harvesting)
Vulnerability: What psychological lever is pulled? (e.g., Authority Bias, FOMO, Panic)
Target: Who is most likely to fall for this? (e.g., Gig Workers, Elderly, General)
[Action] The Application: Provide a highly actionable, 3-to-5 step bulleted checklist of what the user should do right now to protect themselves or recover.
[Pause] The Assessment: Conclude with 1 to 3 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) to test knowledge retention before the user moves to the next module.
