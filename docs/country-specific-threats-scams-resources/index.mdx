# Country-Specific Threats, Scams & Resources

*The scam that targets someone in Mumbai looks different from the one targeting someone in Manchester. The law that protects you depends on where you are. So does the number you call.*

---

## The Wrong Helpline

Priya lost Rs 4.2 lakh to a digital arrest scam in February 2024.

The caller said she was linked to a money laundering case. He had her Aadhaar number. He used official-sounding language and a fake police ID. He kept her on video call for six hours.

After it was over, she searched online for where to report it.

**She found three different numbers, two of which had been discontinued.**

She eventually reached the right portal - cybercrime.gov.in - on day three. The 24-hour window for UPI reversal had closed 60 hours earlier.

**The scam was not the only thing that failed her. The information about what to do next was not where she needed it.**

This section exists to fix that. Each module covers the scams most active in a specific region, the laws that apply, and the exact contacts to use - before something happens.

## What Every Module in This Section Covers

Each country module follows the same structure:

- The scam types most active in that region right now
- How they work and why they are effective locally
- The specific laws that apply
- The exact portals, helplines, and agencies to contact
- Time-sensitive steps - because recovery windows close fast

**The modules are not mutually exclusive.** If you live in the UK but your family is in India, both modules are relevant to you.
