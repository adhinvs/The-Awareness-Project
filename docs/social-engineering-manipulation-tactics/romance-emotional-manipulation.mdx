# Romance & Emotional Manipulation

*Before anyone steals your money or your dignity, they steal your trust. This module explores how bad actors engineer intimacy, manufacture affection, and weaponise loneliness to gain total control over another person.*

---

## The Hook: The Man Who Felt Real

Meera was 54 years old, recently divorced, and living alone in Pune for the first time in her adult life.

She had joined a Facebook group for people learning watercolour painting. It was just a hobby. A way to fill the evenings.

**A man named "James Harrington" sent her a friend request.** His profile showed a handsome, silver-haired civil engineer working on infrastructure projects in Aberdeen. He had photos of weekend hikes, a golden retriever, and what looked like a close-knit family.

He had noticed her artwork in the group. He left a kind comment. Then a private message.

![A person sitting alone near a window, lit softly by daylight](/img/social-engineering-manipulation-tactics/romance-emotional-manipulation/person-lonely-window.jpg)

**Within three days, he was messaging her every morning.** "Good morning, Meera. I hope your brushes are ready." It felt warm. Playful. Different from anything she had felt in years.

**Within three weeks, he told her he was falling in love with her.** He said he had never met anyone who understood him the way she did. He talked about retiring early and moving to India. He asked which city she thought was best for a quiet life together.

Meera told her sister. Her sister was worried. Meera told her sister she was being paranoid.

**Six weeks in, James hit a problem.** His construction equipment had been seized at a port in Malaysia due to a paperwork dispute. He needed $4,200 to release it. Just for ten days. He would return it the moment he landed in Pune.

Meera sent it.

**Then there was a customs fee. Then a medical emergency.** Then a wire transfer that "bounced" and needed to be re-sent. Over four months, Meera transferred 22 lakh rupees to various accounts. Her savings. Her emergency fund. A portion of the settlement from her divorce.

The day she ran out of money, James disappeared.

There was no James Harrington. There never had been. His photos had been generated by an AI system that creates consistent, believable faces that do not belong to any real person. His messages had been scripted by a team of operators running dozens of "relationships" simultaneously from a compound in Southeast Asia.

Meera had not been naive. She had been targeted by professionals.

## What Is Actually Happening: The Architecture of Fake Love

These are not opportunistic scams. They are engineered systems.

Every step of what happened to Meera has a name, a method, and a statistic behind it.

### Catfishing, fake personas, and AI-generated identities

A **catfish** is a person who creates a fictional identity online to deceive another person into a relationship. The method has existed for decades. What has changed is the technology behind it.

Traditionally, catfishers stole photos from real people's social media profiles. They were catchable. A reverse image search could expose them in seconds.

That window is closing.

**AI can now generate photorealistic portraits of people who do not exist.** Consistent across multiple angles, ageing naturally across a fabricated timeline of photos, completely absent from any image database. A reverse image search finds nothing, not because the person is clean, but because they were never real.

> "AI has taken the traditional dating scam and put it on steroids. Criminals can now generate realistic photos, polished profiles and convincing conversations at scale."
>
> Source: Haywood Talcove, CEO, LexisNexis Risk Solutions Government, 2026

Beyond photos, scammers now use AI chatbots to maintain dozens of fake relationships at once. Each one appears personal. Each one remembers details. **Only 46% of people correctly identified AI-generated photos** in a controlled test. (Source: Norton Cyber Safety Report, 2025)

The result is a scam that passes every casual check a person might reasonably make.

### Love bombing - overwhelming affection as a control tactic

The technical name for what James did to Meera in those first weeks is **love bombing**.

It is a deliberate psychological tactic. The goal is not affection. The goal is dependency.

When someone floods you with constant attention, daily messages, declarations of love within days, and talk of a shared future before you have even met, they are not expressing genuine feeling. They are building a trap. The trap is emotional debt. The more invested you feel, the harder it becomes to question what is happening.

The pattern always follows the same arc:

**Idealisation.** You are perfect. They have never felt this way. Every word you say is fascinating.

**Devaluation.** Once the bond is secured, the warmth cools. They become distant, critical, or hard to reach. You feel confused and desperate to get back to how things were.

**Repeat or discard.** They either leave (having taken what they needed) or return to warmth, restarting the cycle. Each return pulls you deeper in.

This cycle, idealise and then withdraw, is the engine of emotional control. It keeps people chasing something that was never real.

### Pig butchering - long-term trust before financial extraction

What happened to Meera has a specific name in the scam industry. It is called **pig butchering**, from the Chinese phrase meaning "fattening a pig before slaughter."

The name is brutal because the method is brutal. Build trust over weeks or months. Make the relationship feel real. Then introduce a financial opportunity, an investment, a crisis, an emergency, a loan. Repeat the extraction until the victim has nothing left.

The scale of this is difficult to comprehend.

![An infographic showing the stages of Pig Butchering Fraud: friendly social media contact, growing investment charts, requests for more funds, and ultimately lost funds](/img/social-engineering-manipulation-tactics/romance-emotional-manipulation/crypto-scam-warning.jpg)

**The numbers behind it:**

- **$5.8 billion** was lost to pig butchering investment fraud in the US alone in 2024. (Source: FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center, IC3 Report, 2025)
- Global losses over four years are estimated to exceed **$75 billion**. (Source: University of Texas at Austin, Griffin and Mei, 2024)
- **US victims lost an average of more than $150,000** each in confirmed cases. (Source: TRM Labs, 2025)
- People aged 60 and older reported more than **$2.8 billion** in losses in a single year. (Source: FBI IC3 Report, 2025)
- Investment fraud losses grew **66% year-on-year** in 2024. (Source: FBI IC3 Annual Report, 2025)

Meera was not an outlier. She was statistically average.

There is also a human trafficking dimension to this that most people do not know. Many of the people sending these messages are not free agents. Hundreds of thousands of people across Southeast Asia have been trafficked into scam compounds and forced to run these operations under threat of violence. (Source: United Nations, 2024)

### Isolation from friends, family, and sceptics

Notice what Meera's sister did. She raised a concern. Notice what Meera did. She dismissed it.

That is not coincidence. That is the design.

Isolation is not a side effect of emotional manipulation. It is a primary tactic. If the people who know you best are still in your life, they might ask the questions that break the spell. So they have to go.

It happens gradually and it rarely looks like control. It looks like love.

"You spend so much time with your sister. Don't you want to talk to me?" A small comment. A slight withdrawal of warmth when you make plans without them. A gentle criticism of a friend: "She sounds like she doesn't want you to be happy." Over time, your circle shrinks. Your world narrows. The only person left who knows everything about you is the one running the operation.

### Loneliness and sympathy exploitation

Scammers do not pick targets randomly. They look for signals.

**Loneliness is the most targeted vulnerability.** People who are recently divorced, widowed, living alone, or going through difficult transitions are disproportionately targeted. Not because they are weak. Because they have a genuine human need for connection that is currently unmet, and that need is visible.

Meera joined a hobby group. She was new to living alone. She was visibly navigating a life transition. From a targeting perspective, she was searchable, reachable, and emotionally open.

Sympathy exploitation works alongside loneliness. Once the emotional bond exists, the scammer introduces a crisis that triggers your empathy. A sick parent. An equipment dispute. A frozen bank account. A missed flight. Each crisis is designed to make you the person who helps. Because good people help. And scammers know that.

## Why This Works on Anyone

It is important to say this clearly.

**Intelligence does not protect you from this.** Emotional need is not a flaw. It is the most human thing there is. Scammers are not exploiting stupidity. They are exploiting the part of you that wants to love and be loved. That is not something to be ashamed of. It is something to understand.

**Americans lost an estimated $3 billion to romance scams in 2025**, up from $1.2 billion the year before. (Source: LexisNexis Risk Solutions Government, 2026)

**1 in 4 online daters globally** has been targeted by a dating scam. (Source: Gen Digital Cyber Safety Report, 2025)

Norton blocked more than **17 million dating scam attacks** in just the final three months of 2025 alone. (Source: Norton, 2025)

These are not rare crimes. They are industrial operations.

## What That Just Showed You

The pattern across every tactic in this module is the same.

### Manufactured intimacy is faster and more convincing than real intimacy.

Real relationships build slowly. You meet someone's friends. You see them in different moods. You learn their inconsistencies. Manufactured intimacy skips all of that. It presents only warmth, only attentiveness, only exactly what you need. That perfection, not imperfection, is the first warning sign.

### Financial requests always come after emotional investment, never before.

Pig butchering works because by the time money is mentioned, you are already emotionally committed. You have already told this person things you have never told anyone. You already believe they love you. Saying no to the money feels like saying no to the relationship. That is the mechanism.

### Isolation is a transaction, not a feeling.

When someone pulls you away from the people who know you, they are not being possessive because they love you deeply. They are removing the people most likely to end the operation. Anyone who makes you feel that your existing relationships are a threat to your new one is giving you information about their intentions.

## Three Things Worth Doing

You do not need to stop trusting people. You need to keep the right people in the loop.

### 1. Maintain a "suspicious of perfection" reflex.

A person who is always available, always warm, always perfectly attuned to your emotional state, and never has a bad day is not a perfect match. They are a performance. Real people have texture. Be more curious about people who seem too perfect than people who are obviously flawed.

### 2. Treat any financial request from an online relationship as a full stop.

It does not matter how long you have been talking, how much you care, or how real the crisis sounds. The moment money enters an online relationship that has not involved a verified, in-person meeting, treat it as a manipulation attempt until proven otherwise. The amount does not matter. Small requests are tests. They escalate.

### 3. Keep one person in your life who knows everything.

The most powerful protection against emotional manipulation is someone who knows you, knows the relationship, and has permission to say "something feels wrong here" without you shutting it down. That person is your early warning system. Keep them close.
