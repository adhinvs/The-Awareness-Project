Rahul Mehta
Civil Engineer · Frankfurt, Germany
Profession
Senior Civil Engineer on an infrastructure project abroad
Explains why video calls are impossible ("poor connection on-site"). Establishes credibility and wealth. Creates a reason for emotional loneliness that makes romantic attention more believable.
Photo
AI-generated face. Professional, attractive, age-appropriate.
AI-generated photos don't appear in reverse image searches. Real stolen photos can be found. The face is designed to be attractive but not implausibly so - too perfect triggers suspicion.
Backstory
Widower with one child. Parents deceased. Limited family contact.
Creates emotional vulnerability and need for connection. Explains why nobody can verify his existence. The limited family reduces the chances the victim will suggest "let's call your sister."
Communication style
Warm. Remembers details. Texts every morning. Never pushes.
Scripted behaviour that mimics genuine emotional investment. "Remembering details" is often a copy-paste from earlier in the conversation. Consistency over weeks builds the emotional attachment that makes the eventual ask feel like betrayal of trust - which is why victims hesitate to disengage.
Video calls
Always delayed. Poor connection, security policy, time zone.
The worker running this persona may be managing 20+ relationships simultaneously. Live video is impossible. The excuses are designed to be individually plausible while never actually delivering a call. If pressed, a pre-recorded video may be used.
Investment mention
Arrives in week 9. Framed as personal sharing, not a pitch.
The timing is deliberate. By week 9 the emotional investment is significant. The suggestion is framed as "I thought you might want to know" rather than "I want your money" because the victim is more likely to engage with something that feels like intimacy rather than a sales pitch.

This is what the first weeks look like. The persona is designed to feel real. See what comes next.

What This Showed You

  • Every element of the persona was chosen to prevent verification and maximise emotional investment.
  • The investment suggestion is never the first ask - it arrives after weeks of warmth.
  • A single live video call within the first two weeks exposes the scam immediately.
  • One conversation with a sceptical friend almost always breaks the illusion.
  • Recovery from pig butchering involves grief for a relationship that felt real, not just a financial loss.
