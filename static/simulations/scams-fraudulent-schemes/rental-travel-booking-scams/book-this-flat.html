Run the 3-Minute Verification

0 of 4 checks complete
🔍
Reverse image search the property photos Right-click a listing photo and run a Google Images search. Do the same photos appear at a different address?
Listing B photos are cloned. The same photos appear on a legitimate listing in a different city. The property exists - but the person advertising it does not own or manage it.
📅
Check the website or platform domain age Paste the booking website URL into a WHOIS tool. How long has it existed?
Listing B's website was registered 6 weeks ago. A site claiming to offer "years of trusted bookings" with a 6-week-old domain is fraudulent. Platform A's domain is over 10 years old.
💳
Check the payment method requested Is payment required through the original platform, or via bank transfer/WhatsApp?
Listing B requests direct bank transfer. This removes all dispute rights and payment protection. The 10% "discount" is the cost of losing your ability to get money back if something goes wrong.
📞
Call the property independently to confirm Find the property's direct number through an independent search. Call and confirm your booking reference.
Listing A can be confirmed. The property's direct number, found via independent search, confirms the booking reference matches platform records. Listing B cannot be confirmed - the number provided goes to a personal mobile, not the property.

Verdict: Listing B is the scam

  • Cloned photos from a real property in a different location.
  • Website registered 6 weeks ago despite claiming years of service.
  • Payment required off-platform, removing all consumer protection.
  • No independently verifiable contact at the property.
  • The 10% discount is the price of losing your deposit entirely.
