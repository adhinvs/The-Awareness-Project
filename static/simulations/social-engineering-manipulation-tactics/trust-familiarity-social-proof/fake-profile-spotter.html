MI
Meera Iyer
Senior Investment Partner · BlueSky Capital Partners
FAKE
  • ⚠
    Exactly "500+" connections LinkedIn shows the exact number up to 500, then switches to "500+". A legitimate profile with 500+ connections built over time will often show that precise number once it clears the threshold. A new fake account cannot organically build real connections — so operators use mass connection farms to hit the 500+ display quickly. The vague "500+" on a 2-month-old account is a major red flag.
  • ⚠
    Account created 2 months ago with a senior title "Senior Investment Partner" typically requires 10-15 years of career progression. A profile reaching this title in 2 months of LinkedIn existence has no history to validate it.
  • ⚠
    Zero recommendations A genuine senior finance professional with 500+ connections and years of experience would have at least one specific recommendation from a colleague or client.
  • ⚠
    Generic skills with no specificity "Finance" and "Investment Analysis" are category-level terms, not skills. Real professionals in this space list specific deal types, asset classes, or tools they've actually used.
  • ⚠
    "BlueSky Capital Partners" has no verifiable web presence The firm name is entirely generic. A legitimate PE/VC firm of any size has a website, press coverage, and portfolio company mentions. Search independently before engaging.
  • ⚠
    Latest post is a motivational quote with no substance Real finance professionals share deal analysis, market commentary, or industry observations. Posting motivational quotes with emoji is filler content used to appear active without saying anything verifiable.

Alex Thompson
Global Head of Talent Acquisition · Global Talent Solutions Ltd.
FAKE
  • ⚠
    Overlapping education dates that are impossible Oxford 2017–2021 AND London Business School 2019–2021 AND Harvard Online 2020–2021 are listed simultaneously. No one attends two full-time degree programmes at separate institutions concurrently. This is a common data-entry error on fake profiles built quickly.
  • ⚠
    Account only 3 weeks old A "Global Head of Talent Acquisition" would have a LinkedIn presence dating back years. An account this new for a senior-sounding title is almost always fraudulent.
  • ⚠
    Immediate job offer after connection The first message after connecting is a job offer asking for a CV. Legitimate recruiters do not operate this way. This is a data-harvesting or credential-phishing technique — they want your CV for personal data (address, phone, previous employer names) or to send a malicious attachment as a "position description."
  • ⚠
    "Global Talent Solutions Ltd." is a generic, unverifiable company name Search independently. The name is designed to sound plausible but cannot be traced to a real registered business.
  • ⚠
    Unusually high salary for an unsolicited cold approach "$180K" in an opening message is a FOMO trigger. Real senior roles at this level are filled through referral networks or specialist recruiters, not cold LinkedIn messages to profiles that may not match the requirement.

The pattern behind all fake profiles
Fake profiles optimise for looking impressive and credible at a glance — vague senior titles, suspiciously rounded connection counts, and generic skills that cannot be questioned. Real profiles contain friction: specific institutions, modest career steps, posts that reference real tools and past failures, and connection counts that reflect actual networking rather than a target threshold. When a profile triggers a quick yes, slow down and verify the company and title independently before acting on any request.
