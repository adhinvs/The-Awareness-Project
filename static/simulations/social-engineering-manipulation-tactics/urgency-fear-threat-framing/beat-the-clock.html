What just happened
You handed over everything.
In a real attack, the moment you clicked Submit, the attacker would have captured:
  • Your account number — used to identify and target your account
  • Your net banking password — immediate access to your funds
  • The OTP — the only second layer of defence, now gone
The padlock and "sbi-secure-verify.in" look official. Neither is. The padlock only means the connection is encrypted — it says nothing about whether the site is legitimate.

What made this page feel real

  • ⚠
    Familiar logo and branding Attackers copy official bank styling exactly. The visual is not evidence of legitimacy.
  • ⚠
    Padlock icon in address bar The padlock means encrypted transport only. Phishing sites use it too.
  • ⚠
    Plausible-sounding domain "sbi-secure-verify.in" contains the word SBI but is not owned by SBI. The real domain is onlinesbi.sbi.
  • ⚠
    OTP request on a page you navigated to A real OTP is sent to verify a login you initiated — not to verify your identity on a page you reached via a text message link.
The rule
Never navigate to a bank page from a message.
Find your bank's URL from their official app, the back of your card, or a previous printed statement. Type it manually. The link in the message was the attack.
Good instinct
You broke the loop.
Pausing defeats every urgency-based attack. The deadline has no power once you stop.

What was really behind that countdown

⏱
The timer ran only inside your browser tab. Your bank never knew it existed.
🔌
No server. No real deadline. Nothing would have happened when it hit zero — it would have simply stopped.
⚙️
Any countdown timer can be added to any page in minutes. It is decoration. It proves nothing about the threat being real.
Key insight
Real institutions do not operate on 10-minute windows.
Every major bank, government agency, and court sends written notices with 7 to 30 days to respond. No real financial institution will close your account in under 24 hours without multiple prior notifications. Any deadline that cannot be verified through an independent channel is fabricated.

How to recognise manufactured urgency

  • ⚠
    A deadline under 24 hours Real threats give you time to think. Manufactured ones do not — because verification kills the attack.
  • ⚠
    A link in the message you must click to act If the message contains both the threat and the solution, the solution is the trap.
  • ⚠
    A visible countdown or "offer expires" notice Countdown timers are trivial to add to any page. They prove nothing about the reality of the threat.
  • ✓
    The pause-and-verify rule When urgency arrives, slow down instead of speeding up. The amygdala spike lasts minutes. Outlast it and rational thinking returns. Call your bank directly on their official number — from the back of your card, not from the message.
